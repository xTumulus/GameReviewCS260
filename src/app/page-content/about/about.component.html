Thanks For Visiting

We're Retrospectre, a site dedicated to examining the impact and significance of video games through retrospective reviews. Specifically, we review games taking into special consideration how they have been effected by the passing of time and changes in the video game industry and game medium.

Mission Statement

To encourage the meaningful examination of games by their audiences and to celebrate games of truly lasting value.

About Us

Due to the demanding deadlines of much of mainstream games journalism, it is often difficult for reviewers to truly give the time we feel is necessary to adequately review games. As a bare minimum, each of us will fully complete the main campaign, story, or primary mode of play of any game we review and then allow ample time for reflection. Included with each review will be brief notes on the content the reviewer did fully experience. For our reviews, we try to choose games that we believe should be reexamined after time has passed to better understand their relevance today. A game must be at least two years old to be considered for a review.

Why It Matters

There seems to be little meaningful reflection on videogames today. Currently, release reviews are the standard and secondary reviews only come after significant patches or remastered versions. Very little is done to look at a game's contribution in the time after its release. Further more, many games require significant time investment to get a full experience. This is often not possible when reviewers have only a few days to play and ponder a game. Altogether, the review process has become a bit shallow. The depth of book discussions and film reviews is rarely emulated in the gaming industry. We aim to consider and discuss games in the same way that powerful literature and film are pondered and reviewed today.
